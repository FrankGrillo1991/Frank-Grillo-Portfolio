Why I Simplified My React Projects to Just src/App.js and src/App.css

When I first started building React apps, I thought the more folders and files I created, the more “professional” my projects looked. Like many beginners, I followed the boilerplate structure I saw online: 

src/
├── components/
├── pages/
├── styles/
├── utils/

Even for the simplest single-page app, I’d end up with a dozen files scattered across multiple folders. At first, it felt organized. But as I worked on more small projects, I realized it was overkill. My portfolio was full of React projects that looked bloated on GitHub — and even I had to dig around just to find where I put certain components or styles.

The Problem With Over-Engineering

Over-abstracting too early adds unnecessary friction, especially when you’re building quick demos or MVPs.

  • It slows you down.
  • It makes code harder for others to read.
  • It doesn’t actually add value when the app is small and simple.

My New Approach

So, I challenged myself to streamline my projects. Instead of creating folders I didn’t really need, I stripped everything down to the essentials: 

src/
├── App.js
├── App.css

And that’s it. All the JSX, logic, and styles lived in just those two files. For small apps, that was more than enough. No confusion. No wasted time switching between files. Just clear, readable code.

The Results

  • ✅ Faster to build.
  • ✅ Easier to maintain.
  • ✅ Cleaner GitHub repos.
  • ✅ More approachable for reviewers and hiring managers.

Lessons Learned

You don’t have to use all the tools and patterns just because they exist. Choose the right level of complexity for the job.

For large-scale or team projects, of course, I use a more robust structure. But for portfolio demos, personal experiments, and MVPs, my minimalist approach has been a game changer.

Final Thought

Simplicity is a skill — and in React (and in web development in general), less is often more.

If you’re just starting out, don’t be afraid to keep your projects small and focused. Build what you need, and nothing more.
